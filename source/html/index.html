Beethovens Werkstatt Modul 2
Version $$git-short$$, with uncommited changes
This prototype is work in progress, and may change at any time. If you want to report feedback, please make sure to include the relevant git commit hash ($$git-short$$).
The purpose of this prototype is to try out concepts for the second module of our project, which deals with Beethoven as an arranger of his own works. While we tried to trace genetic processes in manuscripts in our first module, we now seek to find such traces in printed texts: How did Beethoven arrange his works for other performing forces? To which degree does he keep the music untouched? These and other questions are explored by comparing the musical texts of re-arrangements and their template works.
What you see here is an unprotected view into our workshop. Neither the data or software nor the concepts manifested by them are our final take on this module. It's just one aspect we'd like to try out. Of course, feedback is highly appreciated and helps us to evaluate various approaches.
